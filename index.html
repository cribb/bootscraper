The Age of Clipper Ships

Clipper ships were fast sailing vessels of the 19th century, built for speed on long ocean routes. Their tall masts and vast areas of sail let them race across the seas carrying tea, wool, and gold.

Most clippers had narrow hulls, a sharp bow, and a large rig. They sacrificed cargo space for speed, hoping to beat competitors to market by days or even weeks.

Famous Clippers

Ships like the Cutty Sark and the Flying Cloud became legends for their record-breaking voyages around Cape Horn and the Cape of Good Hope.
